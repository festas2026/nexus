NEXUS

"NEXUS" is the title of the first edition of the FESTAS art and science festival, which will be held from June 22nd to 26th, 2026, in the historic center of Naples. NEXUS represents the connections, ties, relationships, and intersections between themes and disciplines, between sensory and cerebral experiences, between emotions and knowledge, between people, across time and space.

NEXUS is a journey to the places where art and science collide, an exploration without a map, without a predetermined itinerary, guided by five key words: Nature, Environment, 21st Century, Humanity, Society. A skewed acrostic that will inspire artists and scientists in their productions and engage spectators.

NEXUS is the first experiment of FESTAS, a series of events that examines the complexity of the world we live in, and the world that awaits us, from multiple perspectives, exploring the connections between human activities and the environment, between nature and society, without facile simplifications, with awareness, and with emotion.

FESTAS aims to stimulate active and informed citizen participation through the exploration and valorization of the expressive potential of art to address complex and socially relevant scientific issues. This first edition of the festival will feature a small number of artistic productions, in various locations around the city, each with its own aesthetic and scientific value (a theatrical performance, a contemporary art installation, a photography exhibition, an audiovisual performance, an artistic workshop, a debate), aiming to spark the interest of a diverse audience, including the local artistic and collecting communities.

The HuT

The first edition of FESTAS will coincide with the final assembly of the European project The HuT – The Human-Tech Nexus (https://thehut-nexus.eu/), funded by Horizon Europe and coordinated by the University of Salerno. The forty-eight-month project is exploring the relationship between governance, human behavior, and technology in managing weather-induced risks, amidst a growing climate emergency. FESTAS aims to evolve, in festival form, activities at the intersection of science and art that have already developed within The HuT project.
